Ruth O. Saxton is a professor of English and cofounder of the women's studies program at Mills College in Oakland, CA, where she has been recognized for exemplary teaching. She edited The Girl, Constructions of the Girl in Contemporary Fiction by Women, and coedited Approaches to Teaching Woolf's Mrs. Dalloway (with Eileen Barrett) and Woolf and Lessing: Breaking the Mold (with Jean Tobin). She has recently finished a collection of essays about the aging female protagonist in contemporary fiction.
